Principles

Working & living principles

Not rules, but defaults. I expect them to evolve as I learn more.

  • Bias toward shipping. If something can be shared in a week, I don’t let it sit for a month. Small shipped projects beat big unfinished ideas.
  • Start from the user, not the feature. Whether in product or investing, I try to first understand the person — their pain, workflow, and constraints.
  • Write to think. When I’m confused, I write. Clear writing forces clear thinking and better decisions.
  • Long-term games, long-term people. Careers compound. I prefer to work with people I can imagine collaborating with for a decade.
  • Data-informed, not data-paralysed. Numbers should sharpen judgment, not replace it. When the sample size is small, I listen harder to qualitative feedback.
  • Default to kindness and clarity. Direct, honest feedback delivered with respect beats vague politeness.
