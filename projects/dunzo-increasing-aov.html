Dunzo Daily — Increasing Average Order Value (AOV)

Summary: Dunzo Daily is a convenience delivery service that aims to deliver daily essentials within 19 minutes. The service has two key customer groups: those who order small, frequent baskets (low AOV) and those with larger, planned baskets (high AOV). This study outlines a product strategy to increase AOV by 30–40% among two key customer segments—low- basket and mid-basket users—using targeted nudges, replenishment triggers, and tailored merchandising.

Understanding the Customer Segments

Low AOV segment includes customers who order items worth ₹150–₹200 per order. These users typically order 3–4 small items at high frequency (7–8 times a month). They usually purchase products like milk, bread, eggs, and fruits.

Mid AOV segment includes customers who purchase items worth ₹300–₹400. These customers order 5–6 items and combine essentials with indulgence products.

Objective

Increase AOV by 30–40% for both low- and mid-basket customers by encouraging them to add more items through:

  • Personalised nudges
  • Replenishment suggestions
  • Optimised home screen merchandising
  • The incorporation of unit economics-driven bundles

Strategy

Segment 1: Low AOV (₹150–₹200)
The idea is to nudge these customers with personalised suggestions based on their previous orders. Since they typically order 3–4 daily essentials regularly, replenishment-based reminders can be used to increase their overall basket value.

Approach

  • Introduce “Frequently Bought Together” suggestions on the home screen and checkout page
  • Utilise replenishment triggers like “You last ordered milk 4 days ago”
  • Add personalised coupons such as “Get ₹20 off when you buy 2 more items”

Illustration

Segment 2: Mid AOV (₹300–₹400)

Mid-tier customers often mix essentials with indulgence or impulse purchases. These users can be nudged to add more items through strategic category placement and price thresholds.

Approach

  • Promote value-pack bundles like “Buy 2 Get 1 Free”
  • Highlight complementary categories (e.g., fruits + snacks, dairy + bakery)
  • Use cart-threshold-based incentives: “Add items worth ₹50 more to save ₹20”

Conclusion

This product strategy aims to increase AOV across low and mid-tier customers by tapping into behavioural nudges, personalised recommendations, inventory replenishment cues, and optimised merchandising. A combination of these approaches can help increase the average basket size by 30–40%, significantly improving Dunzo Daily’s unit economics and customer retention.
