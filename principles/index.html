Principles
  • Bias toward shipping.

    If something can be shared in a week, don’t let it sit for a month. Small shipped projects beat big unfinished ideas.

  • Start from the user, not the feature.

    Understand the person — their pain, workflow, and constraints — before designing product or recommending investments.

  • Write to think.

    When confused, write. Clear writing forces clearer thinking and better decisions.

  • Long-term games, long-term people.

    Careers compound. Prefer collaborators you can imagine working with for a decade.

  • Data-informed, not data-paralysed.

    Numbers should sharpen judgement, not replace it; use qualitative feedback when sample sizes are small.
